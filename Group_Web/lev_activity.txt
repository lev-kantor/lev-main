Theory of Metastable Impact Electron Spectroscopy (MIES) 

http://www.cmmp.ucl.ac.uk/~lev/researc4/node17.html

In MIES experiments a beam of slow excited (or "metastable") He atoms is
used to probe the electronic structure of crystal surfaces: due to interaction of
the projectiles with the surface a number of Auger transitions is taking place
which are responsible for the observed electron emission. We are developing a theory
of MIES based on state-of-the-art electronc structure methods.

Energy dissipation in Atomic Force Microscopy 

http://www.cmmp.ucl.ac.uk/~lev/researc4/node18.html

Using methods of non-equilibrium statistical mechanics (such as Zubarev's
Non_Equilibrium Staistical Operator Method) we study theoretically
energy dissipation mechanisms in the Atomic Force Microscopy.

Diagrammatic methods in the Theory of Electronic Separability 
and cluster embedding                           

http://www.cmmp.ucl.ac.uk/~lev/researc4/node3.html


We develop diagrammatic methods to study electronic and phonon structure of systems
which can be represented as a set of weakly overlapping units (e.g. atoms, molecules, bonds).
This theory is applied to derive "interatomic" interaction in such materials as well as 
in formulating "rigorous" embedding schemes which could be used in materials of various
chemical bonding to study point defects.

LEV00: User-friendly package for plane-waved DFT codes CASTEP/CETEP/VASP

http://www.cmmp.ucl.ac.uk/~lev/codes/lev00/index.html

This is a set of menu-driven programs which help to work with 
a number of plane wave Density Functional Theory computer codes like CASTEP,
CETEP and VASP. One can produce contour maps of electronic and spin densities,
calculated charges, total, projected DOS and a lot more.
