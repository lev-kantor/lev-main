Vibrations of MgO crystal

 
 
 
 
 

Here we shall obtain vibrations of bulk MgO using the VASP code. This will be done in several k-points, and we shall assign each vibration its corresponding k-point. We shall also plot vibrational DOS.

1. Start tetr and read in the file CONTCAR_MgO containing MgO primitive unit cell. Enter species as Mg and O when asked.

2. Once in the main menu, go to Vs, below:

At the moment, we have the bulk MgO primitive cell, correct atomic masses (no need to change). The calculation is performed using frozen phonon method. Two calculations are performed for each degree of freedom which is the default (using the option Nd one can set it also to 1 or 4, but 2 is ok in most cases). Another important option is Da giving the distortion amplitude; we shall use the default value of 0.01.

3. The next step is to define how many k-points we need to calculate the phonons at. This is defined by the cell extension, option Su. Use it with the matrix


        -1   1   1

          1  -1   1

          1   1  -1


corresponding to the crystallographic cell extension. The menu becomes:

4. This supercell is 4 times bigger than the original cell; therefore, it will be able to give us vibrations in 4 k-points, i.e, in the Gamma point and also in three others. Let us first see a general information about orbits of atoms, i.e. sets of atoms which transform one into another by symmetry operations. In this case, these are only the so-called internal translations, i.e. translations of the original primitive cell which are not equivalent to large translations of the extended cell. Do Co, below:

It is seen that we have two orbits, one is composed of 4 atoms of Mg, and another - of 4 atoms of O. Note that our extended cell contains 8 atoms (4 times extension of the primitive cell). Under “Old#’, numbers of atoms of the supercell belonging to each orbit are given.
